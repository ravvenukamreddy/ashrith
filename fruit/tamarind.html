Tamarind

(Tamarindus indica)

The sour heart of South Indian kitchens—tamarind wakes up digestion and gently cleanses as seasons change into summer.

Season: Late winter–summer Best: Feb–May Origin: Tropical Asia

How to Eat / Serve

  • Soak pulp in warm water; strain for rasam/sambar.
  • Make tangy chutney for snacks and chaats.
  • Sweet tamarind (Thailand variety) is great for candies.

Medicinal Values

  • Stimulates appetite; aids sluggish digestion.
  • Mild laxative; supports natural detox.
  • Rich in tartaric acid and minerals.

Health Benefits

  • Balances heaviness as climate turns hot.
  • Helps maintain electrolyte balance with salty-sour dishes.
  • Adds micronutrients while enhancing flavors.
