Amla (Indian Gooseberry)

(Phyllanthus emblica)

The winter immunity hero of Ayurveda—intensely sour but incredibly nourishing; famous in pickles, candies, chyawanprash, and Triphala.

Season: Oct–Feb Peak: Winter Origin: India

How to Eat / Serve

  • Slice and soak in salt water to reduce sharpness; eat with a pinch of chilli.
  • Make amla murabba (sweet preserve) or add to chutneys.
  • Dry slices for candies or herbal teas.

Medicinal Values

  • Very high in vitamin C; supports immunity and skin health.
  • Included in Triphala for digestion and detox.
  • Antioxidants support hair and eye health.

Health Benefits

  • Great winter support against colds and fatigue.
  • Helps iron absorption from plant foods.
  • Balances metabolism during cold/dry months.
